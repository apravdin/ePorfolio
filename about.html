My Methodology

Here are a few more conclusions that I have made, that greatly influence my thoughts, decisions, and actions.

Minimal designs are absolutely fantastic, not only for web design but for everyday life. The way I see it is, with every new day there is more information that we have to process. Since processing information takes time, as a result time is becoming less readily available. By implementing minimal designs it is possible to display the same message through less resources. For example this paragraph could be written as follows:

data takes time to process
minimal designs display same message with less data
thus less time to process same message


Although this example is a fairly extreme case it is not so far off from the truth. Social media has already begun to implement more simple communication protocols, here is great article that compares IM to email and further demonstrates the importance of minimal designs.

Expanding on the idea above, I think that most knowledge is to some degree useless. Due to the sheer amount of information out there and the various situations that can befall someone, it should not be possible to be prepared for all the possibilities. I think we should treat our brain as a cache. Caches are generally small and store only the most relevant information, and so should our brain. Instead of attempting to learn a broad topic it is more beneficial to understand just the underlying concept. I believe that creative and analytical thinking is much more effective than factual thinking since it scales better to solve any number of problems.

Of course there are many more deciding factors in my methodology, but at this point I hope that I demonstrated that I am a very analytical individual. There are virtually no actions that I perform without a significant thought process.
